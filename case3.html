In Pratappur Rural Municipality ward number 7, Jagatpur and Parsa tole, there is a mixed settlement of Dalits, Madhesi and Tharu. In Jagatpur, the number of Mushars is more and the people here are working as laborers and wage earners in India. The same problem exists in Parsa tol. About 42 households are in Parsa tol, while 40 households are in Jagatpur tole. Due to the problem of drinking water and toilets in that tole, people go outside to defecate, road problems in Persa tole, lack of irrigation, agriculture and public awareness are some of the problems existing in that area.

During the discussion in Pratappur Rural Municipality to conduct the program under the orientation program of the seven-phase plan organized by the Health System Reform Project in response to the COVID epidemic conducted in partnership with Financial support from Australian Aid, the U. M. N. and Indreni Social Development Forum, it was suggested to conduct the program in Parsa and Jagatpur villages of Ward 7. A discussion program was held on the process of the phase. In that program, the stages of plan selection, the budget coming to the municipality and the ward, and the problems of that place were discussed and plans were made with the active participation of the participants. How will the citizens of Jagatpur and Parsa villages choose the plan after discussing the process of the seven step planning process? , where to submit? Be able to do that.

The plan made in the program was presented by the people of Jagatpur to the working committee meeting of Karyapalika . During the selection of the ward level plan, the residents of Jagatpur and Parsa submitted their plans to the ward and requested the ward president to address their problems as much as possible. Ward chairperson has also promised to fulfill the budget allocation for the plans submitted by Tole residents, and he will submit the said demand to the planning program of the rural municipality and if the said demand is not addressed from the municipality level, he has promised to address the demand of Tol residents even if it is from the ward level.
