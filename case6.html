Blessing to Laxmi

Laxmi Chaudhary, residence of Pratappur -07, Tangikot is a hardworking women. She has a family of four, a husband and two sons. Unfortunately her older son could neither listen nor speak. They have very few land which is not enough for them to feed year round. Her husband work as a wage labor. The financial condition of their family does not allow them to have a peaceful life.

During the year 2022, the ReLive project was implemented by Indreni in partnership with UMNMDT. Laxmi Chaudhary got membership in a group created by ReLive. The project creates the group by focusing on landless, seasonal wage labor, marginal farmers, single women (having responsibility to take care of family), PWDs, unemployed/jobless youth through COVID pandemic, low income people having less than NPR 6000 per month. With the aim of awaring people on the concept of handling their money better, financial literacy training was conducted to their group. To encourage them to start saving, a khutruke was a distributed to all the participants. After that training she started saving the money by cutting off her unnecessary expenses. She then started small kirana shop from her savings however she could not afford to buy tanki for her shop.

She seek for help from the Relive project. Seeing her hardworking attitude, the project decided to help her. She received the tank worth Rs. 35,000 from the Indreni. She was very happy to have it and her business has now flourished to new level. Nowadays she seems to be busy in her business. Along with that she has also started to grow some vegetables and sell them. She has now enough money to send school to their children. According to her, “I got courage to have shop from the financial literacy training but was not able to extend it to the higher level due to the lack of space for the groceries. Indreni has appeared as a blessings to me. Now, I have the confidence to work as an independent lady”.
